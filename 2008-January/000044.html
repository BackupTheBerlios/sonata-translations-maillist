<rant>
If only every other project handled translations in such a reasonable
and _organized_ manner as this one... I guess this is the price we pay
for the "release often, release early" strategem that seems to have
been adopted by most opensource projects. Sonata and Mirage are
predictable and we get actually notified about the release a week
earlier by email! How convenient is that for us who don't necessairly
have to follow the development process itself! Sometimes even if we
did, things are still unpredictable... Yes, this is a small project
and it's the small projects I'm talking about.

I think at least some developers underestimate the importance of
translations. Yes they all accept them (I'm not talking about GNOME
SVN, this is a different beast), but don't take them into account
before a release takes place.

With Mirage and Sonata I always take extra care with the translation,
because I know this is how it's going to look like for the release. No
last minute source changes that break strings. There's also lots of
time to do it and test it. I know my translation is well made and
adhers to every rule set by the translation team I'm a member of
(Aviary.pl, official Polish team for GNOME SVN and Mozilla Corp.).

In other words, professional approach and professional quality.

We really don't need much, just an email notification a few days
before a release with a string freeze period...
</rant>
