The Updated Arabic file is attached. There are, however,
two--admittedly slightly pedantic--annoying bugs:

1- In the preferences dialog, the string "Display" is both the name of
a tab and the settings heading in bold in the tab itself. In English,
this is perfectly fine, as both the noun and imperative form of
Display are the same. In Arabic however, the tab name should be a
noun, while the word in bold should be in the imperative. Two strings
should be provided, then.

2- Commas should face the other way in Arabic. (،) is used instead of (,) .
